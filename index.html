Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about HVAC services in Sacramento and surrounding areas.

How often should I have my HVAC system serviced?

We recommend having your HVAC system serviced at least twice a year—once in spring before cooling season and once in fall before heating season. Regular maintenance helps prevent breakdowns, improves efficiency, and extends the life of your system. In Sacramento's climate with hot summers and cool winters, this bi-annual schedule ensures your system is ready for peak demand.

What are signs that my AC needs repair?

Common signs your AC needs repair include: warm air blowing from vents, weak airflow, unusual noises (grinding, squealing, or banging), frequent cycling on and off, high humidity indoors, water leaks around the unit, and unexplained increases in your energy bills. If you notice any of these issues, it's best to call for service before a small problem becomes a major repair.

How long does a typical HVAC system last?

With proper maintenance, a central air conditioner typically lasts 15-20 years, while furnaces can last 15-30 years. Heat pumps usually last about 15 years. Factors that affect lifespan include installation quality, maintenance frequency, usage patterns, and local climate conditions. If your system is approaching these ages and requiring frequent repairs, it may be more cost-effective to replace it with a new, energy-efficient model.

How can I improve my home's indoor air quality?

There are several ways to improve indoor air quality: upgrade to high-efficiency air filters (MERV 11 or higher), install a whole-home air purification system, add UV lights to kill bacteria and mold, use a dehumidifier or humidifier to maintain proper humidity levels, ensure proper ventilation, and schedule regular duct cleaning. We can assess your home and recommend the best solutions for your specific needs and budget.
